How does this YouTube Revenue Calculator work?

Our tool uses a simple but accurate algorithm based on the YouTube Partner Program (YPP) monetization model. YouTube pays creators for ad impressions on their videos, and this calculator estimates your share of the revenue based on two key factors: your traffic volume and your CPM.

Please note that YouTube keeps approximately 45% of the advertising revenue, giving the remaining 55% to the creator. Our calculator already reflects realistic estimates of what would actually reach your pocket.

What is CPM and why is it so important?

The CPM (Cost Per Mille) is the amount of money advertisers are willing to pay for every 1,000 ad views on your videos. Not all channels earn the same, even if they have the exact same views. CPM varies drastically depending on:

  • Your channel's niche: Finance, technology, cryptocurrency, or business channels have extremely high CPMs (sometimes exceeding $15 or $20) because advertisers are selling expensive products. Vlog, prank, or gaming channels usually have a lower CPM (between $1 and $3).
  • Your audience's geography: Views coming from the United States, United Kingdom, Canada, or Australia pay much more than views from Latin American countries or Spain, due to the local purchasing power of the advertisers.
  • The time of the year: During the last quarter of the year (Christmas, Black Friday), CPM skyrockets because all brands are aggressively investing in advertising.
